How to read it. Move the credit spread toward $100 and the needle slides to center — the premium you collect on the spread offsets the time decay on the option you bought, so you're barely paying to wait. The payoff: a defined loss if the stock runs against you, a flat zone where you simply keep the spread's credit if it chops, and an uncapped gain if the move finally breaks your way.
